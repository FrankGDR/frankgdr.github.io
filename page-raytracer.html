GPU raytracer with Unity

A raytracer made in Unity that runs on the GPU.

During my internship in computer graphics research at Université de Montréal, I made a simple raytracer inside Unity running on the GPU (written on a compute shader) to make myself more comfortable with important notions of computer graphics. I started with basic ray-spheres intersection and uniform shading, and then with a perfect mirror shading with reflections. Once I finished this, I tackled the problem of diffuse reflection coupled with global illumination. I implemented a lambertian diffuse bidirectionnal reflectance distribution function (BRDF). To achieve this, I had to read about the rendering equation and how to integrate it using Monte-Carlo integration; however, once that was finished, I was already getting nice results. Of course, I also had to implement specular reflections, using a specular Phong BRDF. I learned about importance sampling (I used simple cosine sampling for this project) and its importance to get pretty results without too much noise.

Finally, I implemented a few other things, like anti-aliasing, transparent and emissive materials, and ray-triangle intersection using the Möller–Trumbore algorithm. Of course, in the end, my raytracer isn't really optimized, but it still gives pretty images:
